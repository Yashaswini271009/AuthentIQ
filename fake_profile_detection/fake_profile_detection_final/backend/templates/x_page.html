X (Twitter) Fake Profile Detection

  • Fake X profiles retweet spam links from fake news outlets.
  • Usernames are often long or random with no real meaning.
  • Fake bios overuse hashtags and emojis to appear trendy.
  • Profile pictures are often avatars or celebrity photos.
  • Accounts may tweet controversial political spam or crypto bait.
  • Low-quality replies and retweets are a red flag.
  • They post inhumanly fast — hundreds of tweets per day.
  • Fake bots reply “DM me” under viral tweets to scam users.
  • They often impersonate journalists, agencies or influencers.
  • New accounts posting heavy opinion or promotions are suspicious.
  • AuthentIQ tracks reply/follow ratios, API behavior and tweet patterns.
  • We detect GPT/AI-written threads and reused tweet chains.
  • Many fake X profiles link to Telegram, adult or promo sites.
  • Imposter accounts use l instead of I, or 0 instead of o (e.g., ElonMu5k).
  • AuthentIQ flags suspicious behavior in real time.
