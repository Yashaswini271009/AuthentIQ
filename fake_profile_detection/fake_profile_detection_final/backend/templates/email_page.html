🚨 Suspicious Email Account Detection

  • Over 3.4 billion scam emails are sent *daily* — and rising.
  • Gmail, Yahoo, Outlook & even fake company domains are common.
  • Scammers often replace letters: e.g., `amaz0n.support@mail.com`.
  • Emails may look legit but use spoofed “display names” to trick you.
  • Phishing emails urge "urgent action" to bypass your critical thinking.
  • Most scams use attachments like invoices, resumes, or fake bills.
  • Cloned login pages steal your credentials without you realizing it.
  • 🧠 Some scammers still use the “Nigerian prince” scheme — since 1988!
  • Check the sender domain — not just the name!
  • Fake support or CEO requests are often loaded with typos or odd tone.
  • Never download random files from unknown senders — especially `.zip`, `.exe`, or `.xlsm`.
  • Use 2FA (two-factor auth) — even if your email looks secure.
  • CAN-SPAM (USA) gives you legal right to opt out of emails.
  • GDPR (EU) protects users from deceptive or unsolicited messages.
  • India’s IT Act 2000 & UK’s Computer Misuse Act prohibit email fraud.
