🕵️‍♂️ Profile Email Analysis: How We Check

This detector uses a set of rules to analyze email characteristics. It's not foolproof but provides a good indication.

1. Email Domain Check

We check if the domain is from a known trusted provider (e.g., Gmail, Outlook), a common provider, a known disposable (temporary) email service, or an uncommon/unknown domain.

2. Email Format & Syntax

Ensures the email follows a valid structure (e.g., username@domain.com).

3. MX Record Check

Verifies if the domain is configured to receive emails. Missing MX records are a strong indicator of a non-functional or fake email.

4. Username Analysis

Looks for suspicious keywords (e.g., "temp", "spam"), unusual patterns (e.g., "aaaaa", "asdfasdf"), excessive numbers, or gibberish-like usernames.

5. TLD (Top-Level Domain) Check

Identifies the country or type associated with the domain extension (e.g., .com, .co.uk, .xyz). Some TLDs are more commonly used for spam.

📊 Understanding the Fake-O-Meter & Verdict:

The "Fake-O-Meter" percentage and verdict are based on the accumulated penalty points:

  • 0-39% (Low Risk / ✅): Appears legitimate. Few to no suspicious flags.
  • 40-74% (Medium Risk / 🧐): Possibly suspicious. Some characteristics warrant caution.
  • 75-100% (High Risk / 🚩): Very likely fake or high-risk. Multiple strong indicators found.

The "Total Penalty Score" shows the raw sum of penalties. A score of 0 is best.
